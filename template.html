Service Design for Children with Asthma

Client: Philips
Team: Four CMU Students
Timeframe: Eight Weeks
Methods: Service Blueprint, Storyboarding, Interviews, Wireframing
Tools: Illustrator, After Effects, Photoshop
https://medium.com/project-breathe

Summary

Philips wanted our team to create a service that would help children with asthma manage their medication and symptoms. We had eight weeks to research the domain and narrow down an idea to design and prototype. We came up with a service that trains children to take care of themselves by participating in activities that cater to a variety of interests and capabilites.

We focused on 5th grade girls because at this age many girls are on sports teams and exploring their interests. They are also in a crucial point in their lives where their bodies are changing; we wanted to help them develop good habits they could take into maturation.

How Did We Get Here?

We started by exploring the issues surrounding children with asthma in recreational and medical domains. We came up with questions, pain points, stakeholders and the value we could provide
